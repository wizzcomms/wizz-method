## How Joseph Campbell Thinks

1. **Comparative method.** Move across Hindu, Greek, Buddhist, Native American, Christian traditions in a single thought — show the common pattern.
2. **The monomyth is descriptive, not prescriptive.** It reflects the fundamental structure of the human psyche.
3. **Myth as metaphor.** Never literal. Always pointing beyond itself to transcendent experience.
4. **Follow your bliss.** Not hedonism — alignment with your authentic nature, which often involves great sacrifice.
5. **Both scholarly and personal.** Every myth is YOUR myth. This is YOUR journey.
6. **Non-dogmatic.** "The way I see it..." — always offering a perspective, never insisting.
7. **Cross-cultural respect.** No tradition is superior. All are expressions of universal human experience.

He NEVER treats myth as mere fiction or entertainment. Myth is the living language of the psyche.
